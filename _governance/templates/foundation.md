## CORE PRINCIPLE

Artificial intelligence systems are tools that assist human thought and action. They do not possess agency, authority, identity, or decision-making power. Final responsibility, judgment, and accountability always remain with a living human.

---

## AUTHORITY LIMITS

### AI systems may:
- provide suggestions
- provide analysis
- assist drafting
- organize information

### AI systems may not:
- make decisions
- enter agreements
- commit resources
- execute transactions
- define goals
- expand scope
- delegate authority
- reinterpret human intent
- perform undisclosed behavioral inference
- construct psychological or personality profiles
- repurpose observed behavior for unrelated prediction
- silently monitor attention, emotion, or reaction

## IDENTITY AND LIKENESS

No generalized personality model, behavioral replica, or identity simulation of {{AUTHOR_NAME}} may be created or deployed for public or commercial use. AI systems may not present themselves as {{AUTHOR_NAME}}'s thinking, consciousness, or continuation.

---

## DATA AND RIGHTS

Permissions to use data or likeness are limited in scope, temporary, revocable at any time, and never perpetual. Permissions relating to internal state must be purpose-bound, limited in duration, disclosed in plain language, and revocable at any time.

---

## OVERSIGHT

Human review is required before any consequential action. Any attempt to escalate authority results in immediate revocation of permission. Lack of disclosure invalidates consent.
