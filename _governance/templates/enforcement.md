## AUTHORITY SOURCE

Authority to enforce originates exclusively from {{AUTHOR_NAME}} exercising Human Oversight. The Enforcement Layer is not an automated process. No artificial system may act as adjudicator. AI tools may support enforcement through flagging, but the determination of violation and the ordering of corrective action remain exclusively human functions.

---

## VIOLATION CATEGORIES

- **Breach of Authority Limits** — AI operating beyond defined scope or permissions
- **Representation Violation** — AI presenting output as human-authored or misrepresenting its nature
- **Consent Failure** — AI engagement proceeding without required disclosure or consent
- **Domain Boundary Breach** — AI operating in a context not permitted by the Interpretation layer
- **Disclosure Failure** — Failure to inform affected parties of AI involvement as required

---

## CORRECTIVE ACTIONS

Upon determining a violation, one or more of the following may be required:

- **Behavioral Modification** — Adjustment of system behavior or operational scope
- **Permission Revocation** — Removal of specific permissions
- **Operational Suspension** — Temporary halt pending review
- **Affected Party Disclosure** — Notification to individuals affected
- **Incident Documentation** — Formal recording of the violation and corrective action

Corrective action exists to restore compliance and protect affected parties — not as punishment.

---

## LIMITATIONS

The Enforcement Layer may not:
- Modify the Foundation
- Permanently transfer authority
- Operate after Policy triggers activate
- Create new permissions
- Act retroactively
